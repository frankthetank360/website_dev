About Me

I Graduated with my Masters in electrical engineering in 2013 from UNH, after successfully defending my Master's Thesis: "Intelligent Aerial Store and Forward Packet Repeater". I have five years of experience in the defense industry, where I worked with a variety of technologies, including electronic warefare, thermal imaging and laser countermeasures. I have extensive experience designing printed circuit boards, coding embedded C/C++, working with VHDL, testing electronics in the lab and integrating commercial hardware with custom designs. I enjoy activities such as surfing, rock climbing, mountain biking, hiking and photography; so much so that I took a gap year in 2017 to pursue these hobbies full time and give myself space to determine what I wanted to do next. At the conclusion of my trip I had a very different perspective on life and my priorities had shuffled drastically. The resultant of this fundamental shift in my beliefs has galvanized me to return to academics; reinvent myself and change my current life trajectory. More than anything I want to return to school to learn as much as possible, work on exciting research and use this new knowledge to teach and motivate others.
